My Top 3 Shows


Kaguya-Sama: Love is War

Two geniuses develop over the top and clever schemes to try and get the other to confess their love first, because to them, love is war, and the first to confess their feelings loses (IMDB).

I really like the chemistry between the characters of the Kaguya-Sama: Love is War. Whenever they are all together, it's just pure chaos and it can be described as comedy gold. The jokes are all really good and unexpected. The anime also knows how to build character and how handle emotional scenes.


The Owl House

Accidentally sent to the world of the Boiling Isles before a trip to summer camp, a teenage human named Luz longs to become a witch, with the rebellious Eda and pint-sized demon King at her aid (IMDB).

The Owl House has one of the best LGBTQ+ representation I've seen out of any show. And that's just one of the reasons why this Disney show is amazing. It also has great world building, easily likeable characters, and smooth animation.


Arcane

Set in utopian Piltover and the oppressed underground of Zaun, the story follows the origins of two iconic League champions-and the power that will tear them apart (IMDB).

Arcane can be watched even by those who have never played League of Legends before. The show tackles many societal issues that our modern world can relate to. Such as corruption, difference between social classes, poverty, child exploitation, drug addiction, and more.
